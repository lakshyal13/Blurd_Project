HOW TO SUPPORT DYSLEXIA PEOPLE

Supporting someone with dyslexia means being patient, encouraging, and focusing on their strengths. Listen without judgment, and give clear, step-by-step instructions. Use tools like audiobooks, speech-to-text software, and visual aids to make reading and writing tasks easier. Celebrate small wins, avoid shaming mistakes, and provide plenty of time for reading or assignments. Consistent encouragement builds confidence, and learning about dyslexia helps you understand and support them better.

TREATMENTS

EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT

Multisensory methods to improve reading and writing.

SKILL TRAINING

Focuses on Phonics and comprehension practice to strengthen language skills.

LIFESTYLE ADJUSTMENTS

Audio books, colored overlays and technology based tools support learning and reduce frustration.
